At Agile 2009, I have several great conversations about hiring, but not *just* hiring- hiring *great* testers. How do you hire great testers?

It's *HARD*, for sure!

I suppose that before I can even think about how to hire one, it would help to *define* one -- at least, what makes a great tester in my personal opinion. I have learned that the software testing world has a huge array of people in it, with an incredibly large spectrum of skill sets.

One thing I have learned is that some of the best testers I have met didn't start out in the software industry at all! Though I did, it has been eye-opening to see people who have come from all kinds of backgrounds. Chris McMahon, for example, was a musician -- and from what I have gathered, a darn good one! I once worked with a tester who had a PhD in Material Science and Engineering -- she studied polymer surfaces (if I remember correctly). Her first software testing job was on a UI-less natural language program (a daunting task for any tester!).
